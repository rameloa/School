Session Introduction

At this stage of our process development, our process is operational, churning out product or delivering services to the market. Our work, however, remains far from complete. The operations manager continues to add value to the firm by identifying means of becoming more efficient. A key means of improving our process is through quality management. Many students make the mistake of thinking that quality management is about ensuring our product or service meets specifications. That is, in fact, not the objective - even though it is sometimes an additional benefit. The objective is to ensure that the process is behaving in a way we expect. As examples, sometimes tools or machines begin to wear out. Statistical process controls help us identify when this occurs.

Learning Objectives

In this module you will

  • determine with a process is "capable" of delivering services or manufacturing products within customer specifications. 
  • identify an appropriate means of measuring process variability (e.g., mean, range, count, or proportion)
  • establish control limits on the process variability
  • determine whether the process is behaving as expected using control charts and the aforementioned control limits
