Session Introduction

When considering the question of how much inventory to stock/order, retail operations likely come to mind first. However, the question can be considered far broader than this. Healthcare operations cannot occur without adequate surgical materials, your vehicle cannot be repaired without the shop stocking sufficient spare parts, and passengers will tend to get cranky if they don't get fed on a long flight. This week, we will consider a classic stochastic model to help us answer this question of how much inventory to buy.

Learning Objectives

In this module you will

  • identify the probability of having sufficient stock to make it through a demand period
  • identify the optimal purchase volume that minimizes the total cost of your inventory policy
  • recognize the impact of "reactive capacity" - or a second opportunity to buy additional goods inside the sales period, albeit at a cost premium
  • recognize the impact of "buy-back" policies for unsold goods
