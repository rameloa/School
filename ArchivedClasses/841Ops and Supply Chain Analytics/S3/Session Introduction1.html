Session Introduction

It's difficult to intuitively appreciate the capacity of large, complex processes. Instead, we will break a process down into its constituent sub-processes, or "stages", often using a process flowchart. We will then identify the capacity of each individual stage as functions of labour and machine resource constraints. Finally, we can then step back to identify the larger process's capacity by considering the most severe limitation imposed by the constituent workstations.

Learning Objectives

In this session you will

  • learn to represent a process using a process flowchart
  • learn to identify workstation and process cycle times
  • learn to identify unit processing time and maximum flowrates (i.e., capacity) at constituent workstations (or "stages")
  • calculate unit processing times as the weighted average where weights are equal to the proportions individual products make up the total work volume (or "product mix")
  • use the flowchart and capacity of constituent workstations to obtain the capacity of the larger process
