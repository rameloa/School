Flow unit The unit of analysis that we consider in process analysis; for example, patients in a hospital or calls to call centre or customers in line at Tim Horton's
Flowrate Also referred to as throughput. Flowrate measures the number of flow units that move through the process in a given unit of time. Example: The plant produces at a flowrate of 20 vehicles per hour. 
Capacity The maximum amount that a resource can produce in a given unit of time. I think of it as the "maximum attainable flowrate".
Flowtime Measures the time a flow unit spends in the process, which includes the time it is worked on at various resources as well as any time it spends in a buffer. We may also consider the flowtime through a process stage (so be sure your understanding of the application). 
Cycle Time The time that passes between two consecutive flow units completing the process. 
Buffer A temporary storage of inventory. It could be as simple as a bin where WIP is stored before being worked on at a process stage. 
Idle Time The time a resource is not processing a flow unit. Idle time should be reduced as it is a non-value-adding element of labour or machine cost.
Utilisation The workload imposed by demand of a resource relative to its available capacity.
Bottleneck The stage with the lowest capacity in the process. This identifies the capacity of the broader process. 
Lot A volume of units separated from other lots by a flow interruption in the process.

These definitions are adaptations from Matching Supply with Demand, 5th Edition, by Cachon and Terwiesch.
