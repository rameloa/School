As you know, forecasting is relevant to a great number of fields. Furthermore, depending on the field, forecasting may be used for a different purpose. Forecasting weather is important to our vacation plans - it may dictate when we choose our holidays. Forecasting the future value of a stock may influence when / if to buy that stock. In operations management, forecasting serves the following purposes:

i)

forecasting expected service rate or production rate is necessary to properly design the production / service system to meet future demand;

ii)

short-term (or near-term) operations planning to meet demand (i.e., how will the system by employed, what and how will resources be allocated to meet demand)

iii)

long-term operations planning to meet demand

The forecasting method employed depends on which of the three items above have motivated your use of forecasting.
